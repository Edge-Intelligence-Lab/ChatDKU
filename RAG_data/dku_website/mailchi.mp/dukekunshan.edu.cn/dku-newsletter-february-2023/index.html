Of those, 3,956 applications came from Chinese students, an increase of about 85 percent over last year, and 3,326 applications were from international students, surpassing the record set last year. Read more

News in Brief

Human-computer interaction conference accepts four DKU papers
 
Four academic papers produced by a Duke Kunshan team and international partners were accepted by a premier international conference on human-computer interaction. The team led by Xin Tong, assistant professor of computation and design at DKU, includes undergraduates Jiaxun Cao and Qingyang He, who major in digital media and art. More than 3,000 papers are submitted each year to the ACM CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems 2023, which has a peer review acceptance rate of 23-28 percent. The current focus of the team’s research is on digital human construction, VR/AR games, VR diagnosis for psychological and physical treatment, humanitarian and inclusive system design.

Care costs for disabled elderly to rise sharply

Long-term care (LTC) costs for older adults with disabilities in China are set to increase to 6.4 percent of GDP by 2050, according to Duke Kunshan researchers. The Global Health Research Center team projected that total LTC costs for home and community-based services would rise from the 2020 level of 0.7 percent. The research was published in the Journal of the American Medical Directors Association.
 

DKU faculty guest-edit special edition of journal 

Duke Kunshan and Duke faculty produced a special issue on China's urban villages, or chengzhongcun, in an Asian studies journal. Working as guest editors, the anthropology and history professors looked closely at several urban villages in Beijing, Shanghai, Nanjing, Zhengzhou and Guangzhou for the journal Positions: Asia critique. Through ethnographic, archival and visual analyses, they examined changes to land, labor and governance relations in the context of rapid urbanization. The DKU faculty serving as guest editors were Nellie Chu, assistant professor of anthropology; Mengqi Wang, assistant professor of anthropology; and Qian Zhu, assistant professor of history, along with Ralph Litzinger, associate professor of cultural anthropology at Duke.

DKU gathers experts from across China for environment seminar

Pollution control and low carbon transition were among the topics discussed at a seminar attended by climate change and environmental policy experts from across China. Duke Kunshan’s Environmental Research Center held the event with the support of the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) and DKU funding for faculty research. Joining the environmental economics seminar were scholars from top Chinese universities in the Yangtze Delta Area including Nanjing University, Fudan University, Shanghai Jiaotong University and many others.
 
New off-campus internship and practice site launched in partnership with KPMG

Duke Kunshan launched a new off-campus internship and practice site in partnership with leading professional services firm KPMG. Vice Chancellor for Government Relations Xiaolin Chang and Director of Career Services He Liu, along with 20 DKU students, visited KPMG’s Suzhou office for a school-enterprise cooperation seminar and to attend the unveiling ceremony of the new site.

Building communities of shared interests

Residence Life launched Living and Learning Communities (LLCs) in fall 2022.
The initiative brings together students with common interests across the themes of art (art / literature / drama / cooking), leadership (growth mindset / leadership enhancement), civic engagement (multiculturalism / values / lifestyle) and wellness (physical / mental / environmental health). Students in each LLC, where small groups live together in designated areas of the residence building and experience tailored programs, are encouraged to organize regular events to reach out to others with similar interests.
 

DKU produces Chinese-English calendar on 24 solar terms

The seasons of Kunshan’s subtropical monsoon climate were celebrated in a bilingual calendar. Duke Kunshan’s Office of Communications and the Student Media Center teamed up to produce the 2023 Chinese Solar Terms calendar, which records the cycle of the four seasons through enchanting campus scenes. The artwork is based on the 24 solar terms that farmers in ancient China developed by observing the course of the sun.

Wuhan University team launches Luojia-3 01 satellite

A Wuhan University-developed satellite which breaks new ground in transmission speeds was successfully launched. Luojia-3 01 is an internet-intelligent, remote-sensing test satellite with capabilities for multi-mode imaging, intelligent processing in orbit, as well as satellite-ground and inter-satellite transmission and communication functions. Mainly developed by Wuhan University, the test satellite launched on Jan. 15. The Luojia-3 01 satellite is the key deliverable for the “integrated demonstration and validation of space-based information network in-orbit processing and real-time transmission”, a major project of the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) under the “Basic Theory and Key Technology of Space Information Network” program. Read more (Chinese)

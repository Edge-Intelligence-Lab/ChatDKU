News in Brief

Students win silver in international synthetic biology competition

A DKU team won silver in the 2023 International Genetically Engineered Machine Competition (iGEM) held in Paris. The team developed novel approach of phage-editing targeting citrus greening pathogen bacteria for application in agriculture. A phage, or bacteriophage, is a virus that infects bacteria and can be used as an antibacterial agent. They won the award in recognition of their strong performance in experimental research, computational modeling and human practice, as well as how they displayed their project and promoted it online. Under the guidance of Linfeng Huang, associate professor of biology, the project was supported by the iNNOVATION iNCUBATOR (Dii) of the DKU Innovation and Entrepreneurship Initiative.

Overseas-educated scholars meet at DKU

Duke Kunshan played host to two Western Returned Students' Association groups.
The second general meeting of the WRSA Kunshan and the founding meeting of the WRSA DKU were held on Nov. 10. The WRSA is an organization of scholars who studied abroad and have since returned to China.

DKU hosts library conference for global universities
 
The DKU library hosted the 9th Sino-Foreign Joint Venture University Library Annual Conference on campus. Over 130 professionals from university libraries and related programs gathered at the new library building for the three-day meeting, which featured two keynote speeches, 17 presentations, two poster sessions and a panel discussion. Participants discussed the trends, innovations and challenges facing libraries at Sino-foreign joint venture universities.

Writing and Language Studio opens new coaching space

The Writing and Language Studio (WLS) opened a new space in the Academic Building for drop-in coaching sessions. The WLS provides one-on-one coaching support in areas such as English academic writing and independent language learning to the entire DKU community.

With the opening of the new space in AB 2101, the WLS is offering walk-in English writing coaching services during selected time blocks each week (the schedule is available on the WLS WeChat account). This initiative aims to accommodate students who may have difficulty booking appointments, enabling them to seek assistance on-site without prior scheduling. The new WLS space will also function as a hub for hosting WLS workshops and events, and house a browsing collection of writing and language learning resources for DKU community members.

Reminiscing at Inaugural Alumni Homecoming Day 

Nearly 150 Duke Kunshan and Duke alumni returned to campus with friends and family for DKU’s inaugural Alumni Homecoming Day on Nov. 18. They reminisced about the old days, met students and faculty and experienced the new Phase 2 campus.

Moderated by DKU Senior Director of Development and Alumni Relations Feng Cheng, the alumni initiated great conversations with Duke Vice President and Vice Provost for Climate and Sustainability Toddi Steelman and other professors of the Duke delegation who visited China in November. DKU Chancellor Yaolin Liu, Vice Chancellor for Academic Affairs Scott MacEachern, Duke Associate Vice President for Development and Alumni Engagement Wendy Kuran, and Professor Youmei Feng also warmly welcomed the alumni.

With the support of Duke China Club, Duke alumni Joe Wu (MBA' 14), Cathy Fang (MBA' 12), Jianping Sun (MBA' 14) and Bill Xue (MBA' 13) shared their career experiences and life insights in a panel discussion with alumni, students and faculty.
 

DKU to hold inaugural invitational sports tournament  

Jointly organized by DKU Athletics and the Office of Student Affairs, the inaugural Duke Kunshan Invitational Sports Tournament will be held on Dec. 2-3, featuring over 600 student-athletes from five universities.

Men and women from DKU, Wuhan University, the University of Nottingham Ningbo China, New York University Shanghai, and Xi’an Jiaotong-Liverpool University will compete in basketball, volleyball, table tennis, tennis, badminton and soccer.

Students organize Women in Leadership Summit

A team of undergraduates organized the Women in Leadership Summit, inviting students and faculty to share their stories and delve into the challenges and opportunities for women leaders in their professional life. 

Guided and supported by staff at the apprenticeship program led by Residence Life, the team spent six months working out detailed plans for the event on Nov. 10, which proved to be a tremendous success.

News in Brief

Sociologist receives Nils Petter Gleditsch JPR award

Hyun Jeong Ha, assistant professor of sociology at DKU, won the Nils Petter Gleditsch JPR Article of the Year Award for her co-authored paper "Regime change and religious discrimination after the Arab Uprisings". The research explores the implications of regime transitions in the Middle East for minority groups and religions. Ha and co-authors Jason Kloeck and Nathanael Gratias Sumaktoyo compared the relative roles of societal and governmental discrimination, building upon Ha's extensive fieldwork in Cairo, Egypt, where she observed and interviewed Coptic Christians.

It's never too late to start exercising, even at 80

A study at Duke Kunshan has shed light on the significant benefits of physical activity for individuals aged 80 and above. Led by professor Lijing Yan's team at the Global Health Research Center, the research published in Preventive Medicine indicates that seniors who remain active or switch from a sedentary to an active lifestyle exhibit increased longevity. The study, using data from the Chinese Longitudinal Healthy Longevity Survey, analyzed 22,463 elderly participants, revealing that engaging in exercise not only extends lifespan but also provides benefits for those with chronic illnesses.

Materials science professor listed in World's Top 2% Scientists

Kwang Leong Choy, professor of materials science at DKU, has been recognized as one of the world's most cited scientists across all disciplines. The 2023 World's Top 2% Scientists list compiled by Stanford University ranks Choy in the leading 180,000 of the more than 8 million scientists worldwide. The widely respected rankings are based on a publicly available database created at Stanford to provide standardized information on scientist citations.

Career Fair to bring 30+ employers to campus

The DKU Spring Career Fair 2024 will welcome more than 30 employers to campus including CITIC, HSBC, PwC, AstraZeneca, Eli Lilly, Mevion, Dell, Xiaomi, KONE and GLG. The employers cover industries such as finance, consulting, biopharmaceuticals, medical devices, internet technology and high-tech manufacturing. Together, they will present over 1,700 internship and full-time job opportunities and half of them have expressed interest in hiring international students.

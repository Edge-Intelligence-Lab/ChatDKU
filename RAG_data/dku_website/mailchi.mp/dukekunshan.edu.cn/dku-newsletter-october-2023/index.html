News in Brief

Health experts converge on DKU for symposium 

To celebrate its 10th anniversary, the DKU Global Health Research Center held an international symposium from Oct. 27-28 on the theme of developing policies to achieve the health-related United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, where attendees discussed progress and strategies for achieving universal health coverage by 2030. The event brought together international and domestic global health leaders, including experts, scholars and practitioners from the World Health Organization, the World Bank, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and academic institutions across China, to share their knowledge, advance evidence-based policy ideas and promote collaboration.

Undergrads publish health research as first authors 
 
Research led by Meichen Yap (Class of 2024), Fei Wu (Class of 2023) and Zhengting He, a master's student at Johns Hopkins University who previously worked in DKU's Global Health Research Center, examined associations between individual resilience and anxiety or depression during the COVID-19 pandemic.
 
Supervised by Professor of Global Health Dr. Lijing Yan, the team identified, critically appraised, and summarized evidence from a range of studies focused on adults, released between Jan. 1, 2020, and April 31, 2022. Published in the Journal of Public Health (Oxford University Press), their study found resilience was inversely associated with depression and anxiety during the outbreak. 

DKU-WHU research forum held on campus

The Duke Kunshan University-Wuhan University Interdisciplinary Research Cooperation Forum was held on the DKU campus, focusing on potential research partnerships in health, economics, humanities, and natural, applied and social sciences. DKU Chancellor Dr. Yaolin Liu, Dr. Scott MacEachern, vice chancellor for academic affairs, Dr. Huanfeng Shen, dean of the School of Resource and Environmental Sciences, and faculty members from a wide range of disciplines at DKU and WHU, attended the Oct. 20 event and discussed potential collaborations.

DKU to host university library conference  

Duke Kunshan Library will host the 2023 Sino-Foreign Joint Venture University Library Conference from Nov. 2-4. The conference will bring together library professionals from Sino-foreign joint venture universities in China to explore the latest trends, innovations and challenges in the dynamic landscape of libraries. The conference theme, “New Horizons, Unwavering Mission,” highlights the adaptations that libraries make to embrace an evolving world while staying true to their core mission. The DKU Phase 2 library officially opened on Aug. 14 this year, occupying a floor area of 12,000 square meters across four levels and a basement. In addition to an increased collection capacity of 300,000 books, the library provides innovative space in support of teaching and learning, including the Liu Shuang Film/Music Appreciation Room, the Data and Visualization Workshop and the AR/VR Lab. The new building also features a traditional Chinese-style tea house and an outdoor garden.  
 
Career Services helps students pursue jobs in various sectors  

Duke Kunshan Career Services organized a series of events this fall, including company, professional, graduate school and alumni information-sharing sessions. CITIC Securities, PricewaterhouseCoopers, Swire and Procter & Gamble Company gave onsite and online talks exclusive to DKU students in support of their career development. DKU’s Office of Career Services has been working with university admissions teams from top academic institutions including Duke, Harvard, Yale, Northwestern University and INSEAD, to bring graduate school information sessions to campus, aimed at providing our students with first-hand application information about their programs.

Existing democracies are marred by long-standing inequalities, deep-seated exclusions, and profound injustices. Many argue, however, that it is possible to create much more “inclusive” democratic societies. This course examines the core ideals behind this hope, the prospects for its realization, and the dilemmas of its pursuit. We look at normative and empirical literatures, drawn from diverse contexts and disciplines. Central questions include: When actors envision a more “just,” “inclusive” democratic society, what precisely are they envisioning? How do they justify their visions? What major strategies have been employed to create more “inclusive” democratic processes, and how effectively have they worked?

This is a introductory course in mathematical models in ﬁnance, centered around the problem of pricing and hedging derivative securities. Due to the uncertainty inherent in the evolution of the stock market, the theory naturally involves probabilistic tools and structures such as conditioning, martingales and Markov processes. To focus on the main idea, we will work in discrete-time setting and a simple model so called binomial asset pricing model for most of the course. In addition, we will also discuss a foreign exchange model, an extension of the binomial model with a second currency and derives the Black-Scholes equation.

During the semester, upon recommendation from the academic advisor, the Dean of Undergraduate Studies may permit a student “for compelling reasons” to withdraw from a normal course load to an underload. Such a withdrawal to an underload is possible only prior to the final four weeks of classes for 14-week long courses and the final two weeks of classes for 7-week long courses. Students are permitted to withdraw to an underload only once in their academic career. The only exception to this policy is when a student is granted special permission to withdraw from a course to an underload for medical reasons (See Medical Withdrawal from a Course)

What are the conditions under which people come to perceive their societies as just or unjust? What effects does living under conditions of injustice have on human psychology? This course will examine these questions through consideration of three topic areas. In Unit I, “Distributive Justice”, asking what justice in distribution of basic human resources consists in. In Unit II, “Social Justice”, exploring some of the psychological influences that contribute to prejudice and oppression, and how those influences shape the psychology of minority groups. In Unit III, “Restorative Justice”, examining the psychological underpinnings of a system of restorative justice that focuses on rehabilitation and reconciliation, and will examine the psychological complexities of instituting such a system under conditions of inequality.

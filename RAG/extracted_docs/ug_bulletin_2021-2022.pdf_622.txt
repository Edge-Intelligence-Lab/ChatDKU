The probation status will be reflected on those academic records used for internal purposes only. Students placed on academic probation must acknowledge their probationary status in writing to the Office of Undergraduate Studies in order to continue at the University. They are also required to meet with the Director for Academic Advising or designee and are expected to seek assistance from campus resources, have their course selection approved by their advisors and meet periodically with a designated academic coach. They may not study away during the probation period. Students are expected to clear their probationary status during the term of probation. In order to do so, they must enroll in 16 credits (plus up to one PE credit), of which no more than 4 credits may be taken on a Credit/No Credit basis, and achieve term and cumulative grade-point averages of at least 2.000.

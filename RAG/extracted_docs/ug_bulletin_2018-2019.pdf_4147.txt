The course examines the foundations of modern China from the Song dynasty onwards. In each period students examine a key issue or theme. In the Song the course focuses on the production of elite society through the Confucian reforms to the examination system; in the Yuan, the course examines the conflict and adaptation between the Sinic and Mongol empires; in the Ming students examine the globalization of trade and its impact on the rise of merchant classes; students then examine the relation between class and ethnicity as China became a multi-ethnic empire in the Qing; finally the course examines the impact of foreign models on the emergence of the modern Chinese state.

Few ideas have impacted world history as has the idea that human beings with different skin colors or physical characteristics are fundamentally different or unequal to each other. Though having some old roots, this idea is largely a modern one. Beginning with the conquest of the America’s, the slave trade, and reaching its high point through colonization of Africa and Asia, as well as the Holocaust, Europeans formulated the idea of a “white race” superior to all other races. This course will trace the history of this idea, how it was used and implemented in various parts of the world, how it was combatted and occasionally overthrown, and how its legacies shape global political debates in the present.

A grade of F or NC (See section Credit/No Credit grading system below) indicates that the student has failed to meet the requirements for the course. The grade is recorded on the student’s record. The course must be repeated and a passing grade earned in order for credit to be awarded. If the student registers for the course again, the second entry of the course and the new grade earned are made on the record, but the first entry is not removed. In such cases, both grades are included in the calculation of the cumulative grade point average, but the credit for only one is counted for term credit requirements or among the 136 credits required for graduation.

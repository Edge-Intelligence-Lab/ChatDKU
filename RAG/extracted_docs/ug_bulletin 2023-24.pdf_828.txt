Residence halls are homes for the students who live there. Many people use the word “dorm” to describe the places students live on university campuses. “Dormitory” is word derived from the Latin for “sleep”. Since DKU's residential facilities are part of something far more impactful and meaningful than sleep alone, DKU uses the term residence hall to describe any of the residential buildings. Each hall is supervised by a full-time staff member, called a Residence Life Coordinator, who also lives in the building. Further, each Residence Life Coordinator supervises a team of Resident Assistants who help students develop a sense of community. Information about each residence hall can be found online: https://www.dukekunshan.edu.cn/campus-life/residence- life/residence-halls/

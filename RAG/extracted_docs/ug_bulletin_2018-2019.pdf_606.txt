Learning outcome assessment is essential for the Student Affairs team to evaluate its work and assess student learning. Student Affairs uses assessment results to improve and enhance the quality of its programs and services, as well as to assess student learning. Student Affairs utilizes various methodologies to evaluate program effectiveness, survey student satisfaction, and assess student learning outcomes. Co-curricular learning assessment is a collaborative effort between Student Affairs staff, faculty, and program presenters to ensure that students meet the learning outcome objectives as established jointly by the presenters and Student Affairs. The Student Affairs team expects students to successfully learn the necessary life skills and abilities that strengthen and develop their personal and academic lives. These students will become involved citizen engaged with the university and surrounding communities, as well as in their future work lives.

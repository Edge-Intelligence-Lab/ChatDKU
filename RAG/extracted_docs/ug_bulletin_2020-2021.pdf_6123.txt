Design Perspectives provides an introduction to design methodology, history, and new emerging critical approaches within creative problem-solving. We will explore the history, topics, concepts and philosophical perspectives that intersect both the aesthetic and functional. Design is a cross- disciplinary discourse between technology and culture. Through the looking glass of modern-day design, we will engage with design perspectives and process from historical movements such as the Bauhaus to the modern day speculative and post-planetary design. We will both discuss the failings of design and its triumphs through specific case studies, situating design in a real-world context. Student design research journals, papers and weekly artifact presentations will focus on the dichotomy of historical design principles, and current and future design trends, to debate how these practices define humanity. Through their assignments, students are guided to think critically of their own practice and contemplate how design will define our future.

Nutrition is becoming ever more central to our understanding of virtually all metabolic processes. Its biological basis offers insight into the mechanisms by which diet influences human health and disease. This course explores the physiological functions and dietary sources of macro and micronutrients and how the nutrients impact the cellular processes in type 2 diabetes mellitus, cardiovascular, stroke, obesity, child obesity Alzheimer’s, food allergies, cancer, stomach ulcers, osteoporosis, Crohn’s disease, kidney and liver disease, linking them to immunology and biochemical pathways.

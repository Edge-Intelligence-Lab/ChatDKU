Philosophical questions arise frequently in sporting contexts, and pursuing those questions can be profitable in both directions: on the one hand, emerging issues in sport often require philosophical engagement in order to make progress on a question of practical importance, while on the other hand, reexamining and deploying concepts and theories in the sporting context can helpfully reshape our thinking. The pivotal concepts at the core of this course are fairness and exploitation. We’ll examine these concepts through a combination of great works in the history of philosophy, contemporary work in analytic philosophy, and recent sporting controversies.

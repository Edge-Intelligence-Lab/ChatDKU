This course examines the development of the Chinese intellectual tradition within the Warring States period (475 – 221 B.C.E). After the decline of the Zhou dynasty, as rival states battled for political supremacy, competing thinkers debated the best way to respond to the ethical and political challenges of the time, creating the classic works of Confucianism, Daoism, Mohism, and Legalism. In engaging the ideas within these works, we will consider not only the texts themselves, but also archaeological and other evidence that further illuminates the evolving historical context in which they were written. Texts will primarily be read in English translation.

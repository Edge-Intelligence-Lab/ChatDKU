Faculty should refrain from engaging in activities which do, or may, create a conflict of interest where employees must choose between advancing personal interests or those of the University. All employees must declare potential conflicts of interest annually. Examples of activities that may lead to a conflict of interest include, but are not limited to: Accepting gifts such as money, entertainment or gifts of substantial value, from students and/or their parents, or from any other third party, when the gift may be construed as an inducement of any kind; offering an honorary or courtesy authorship of a scholarly publication in return for favors; maintaining a relationship with an outside entity such that her/his activities on behalf of the University could be biased by that relationship in a direction that would ultimately provide direct financial benefit to the individual or a family member (see Appendix I).

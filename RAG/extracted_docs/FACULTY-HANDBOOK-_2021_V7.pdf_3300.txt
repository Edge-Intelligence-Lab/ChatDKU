To the extent allowed by law, any information obtained during the research misconduct proceeding that might reveal the identity of human subjects participating in the research shall be maintained securely and confidentially and shall not be disclosed, except to those who need to know in order to carry out the research misconduct proceeding. The procedures outlined below are designed to ensure that charges of alleged misconduct are addressed as judiciously as possible, and that any retaliation against a person bringing an allegation in good faith is strictly prohibited. The objective is to define clearly the responsibility for integrity shared by the Duke community, and to make the preservation of trust more secure.

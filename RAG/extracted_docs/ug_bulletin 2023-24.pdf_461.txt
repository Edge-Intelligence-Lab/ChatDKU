Only in exceptional cases and with special permission of both the academic advisor and the Dean of Undergraduate Studies or designee could a student enroll in an overload (defined as more than 10 credits in a 7-week session or more than 20 credits in a term, exclusive of PE) with the maximum not exceeding 24 credits. In determining whether to approve an overload, the academic advisor and the Dean of Undergraduate Studies or designee will carefully consider the student’s academic history, including grades and the number of courses taken in the past, along with other factors. Students should not count on an overload later to compensate for a previous underload.

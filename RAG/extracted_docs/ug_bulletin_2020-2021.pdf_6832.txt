This course to explore how to extend statistical techniques learned in earlier classes and how to apply them to real-world problems. The course starts with the standard linear model and then explores more generalized models and models that rely on certain assumptions about the underlying data, with a focus on identifying which techniques are appropriate for what kinds of data. We rely heavily on interactive use of R software in applying models to real-world datasets. The class is open to all students but is focused on questions related to social problems - those involving human activity.

Advising is more than the imparting of specialized information; it includes helping students explore and discover the role of education in society and teaching students the skills for active learning and perseverance to reach long-term goals. Through advising, students will learn to formulate important questions about the nature and direction of their undergraduate career. Advisors will confer with students about course schedules and educational experiences, but students themselves are responsible for their academic program, social activities, and for making progress toward an academic degree. Students’ advising needs change as they progress through their academic programs, requiring a dynamic advising partnership that pulls expertise and guidance from across campus faculty and administrators.

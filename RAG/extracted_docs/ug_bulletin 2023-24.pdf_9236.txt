Travel narratives include some of literature’s most influential and popular works, from Marco Polo’s travelogues to Sanmao’s Stories of the Sahara. Travel writing remains, however, a vaguely defined and relatively understudied genre. (What about the Odyssey? Is that a kind of travel writing?) However we choose to define its boundaries, travel writing is fundamentally concerned with encounter and representation. How do we make sense of the new? What are the ethics of writing about others? How does travel allow for critical reflection on languages, histories, and identities? What is the relationship between travel writing and forms of imperialism, nationalism, and globalization?

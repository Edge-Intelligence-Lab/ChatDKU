Course examines contemporary Chinese politics, covering regime institutions and processes, policies and their effects, and the dynamics of political development. Course includes an overview of Chinese political history since the founding of the People's Republic, and including the reform era beginning in 1978. Course addresses the role of the Chinese Communist party and central government, as well as the role of subnational government. Students examine state-society relations and political participation and protest as well as economic and social policy. Course also includes attention to China's international political and economic relations and policy challenges China faces in the future. Examples from the worlds of business, healthcare, and education will help to define the enduring role of Party and government in contemporary China.

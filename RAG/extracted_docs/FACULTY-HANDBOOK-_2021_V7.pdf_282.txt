Tenure track positions are normally filled by faculty with a Ph.D. at the three regular rank tenure track titles of assistant professor, associate professor, or professor. Regular rank faculty without a Ph.D. are commonly appointed at the non-tenure track rank of lecturer or instructor. When such an appointment is made, the faculty member will not begin to accrue time toward tenure until the degree is awarded and he or she has been given a tenure track appointment. Subject to variations in hiring units, initial appointment to a regular rank tenure track position without tenure will be for a term of four years.

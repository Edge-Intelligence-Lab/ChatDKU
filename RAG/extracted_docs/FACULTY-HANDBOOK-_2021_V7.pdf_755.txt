Misconduct in research is defined as fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism. In addition, other practices that seriously deviate from those that are commonly accepted within the research community for proposing, conducting, or reporting research may also constitute misconduct in research. These practices are covered by the Policy and Procedures Governing Misconduct in Research (in Appendix O). As noted in that policy, "misconduct" does not include honest error or honest differences in interpretations or judgments of data.

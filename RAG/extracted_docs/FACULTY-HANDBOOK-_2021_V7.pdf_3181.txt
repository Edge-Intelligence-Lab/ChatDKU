To avoid the possibility of adverse impact on faculty members' private consulting arrangements, the University disclosure form will first request information concerning the basic idea of the invention (what it will do but not how it does it) and the circumstances of its conception and/or development (whether on university time or not, what funding sources were involved, etc.). If the director of the Office of Licensing & Ventures concurs with the inventor that the invention or Supportive Technology is not one in which the University has any interest under the Policy, the remainder of the disclosure form need not be completed.

This course takes students on a journey into the worlds of gender, labor, and mobility as they intersect in dynamic and intimate ways. Topics include: exploration the concept of gender by using anthropological and sociological theories; examination of foundational concepts in political economy, including the state, the family firm, class, labor, migration and accumulation; ethnographic case studies on factory labor, housework, sex work, the service industry and IT work across Asia, Europe, and North America; analyzing how historical and contemporary pressures of industrialization, urbanization, uneven development, and information technology challenge our assumptions about gendered labor and migration; how care work, domestic labor, intimacy, and affect do not fit singularly into cultural categories of feminine versus masculine; public versus private; or individual versus collective domains.
